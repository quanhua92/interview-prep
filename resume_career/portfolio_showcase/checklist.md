# Portfolio Showcase — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to prepare a concise, structured walkthrough of your portfolio for interviews.
Aim to present 3-5 projects in 10-15 minutes total, leaving time for questions.

## Drafting Phase
- [ ] Select your top 3-5 projects that best demonstrate the skills required for the target role
- [ ] Clean up your GitHub profile: pin your best repos, write clear READMEs, remove or archive abandoned projects, add a professional bio
- [ ] Prepare 1-2 writing samples (blog posts, tech talks, documentation) as portfolio pieces
- [ ] For each project, write a one-sentence summary of the problem, your approach, and the outcome
- [ ] Prepare 1-2 screenshots or demos for each project to show visually
- [ ] Identify the technical challenge or interesting trade-off in each project to discuss in depth
- [ ] Write a 30-second opening that frames why you chose these particular projects to showcase

## Refinement Phase
- [ ] Read your full portfolio walkthrough aloud — target 10-15 minutes for all projects
- [ ] Verify each project walkthrough follows the four-element framework: Problem Statement, Technical Approach, Key Challenge, Outcome and Next Steps
- [ ] Check that all demo links work and that projects are original (not tutorial-only with no personal extension)
- [ ] Remove any project that does not directly support the skills needed for the target role
- [ ] Prepare answers for common follow-ups: "What would you do differently?" and "What was the hardest part?"
- [ ] Test your demo or screenshots on the device you will use during the interview

## Practice Phase
- [ ] Deliver the full walkthrough to a peer and ask for honest feedback on clarity
- [ ] Practice navigating to each project quickly without fumbling
- [ ] Time yourself and cut or expand sections to stay within 10-15 minutes
