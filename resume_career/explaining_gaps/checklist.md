# Explaining Gaps — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to prepare honest, confident answers for employment gaps on your resume.
The goal is to address the gap briefly and pivot quickly to your readiness for the role.

## Drafting Phase
- [ ] List all gaps on your resume with exact dates and a one-line honest reason for each
- [ ] Identify what you did during each gap (courses, freelancing, personal projects, caregiving, travel)
- [ ] Write a 2-3 sentence explanation for your largest or most recent gap
- [ ] Prepare a transition sentence that connects the gap to your current readiness
- [ ] Prepare distinct approaches for different gap types: layoff (direct and brief), career change (focus on what you gained), personal absence (emphasize productive activities)

## Refinement Phase
- [ ] Verify each explanation follows the three-part framework: State the fact / Brief reason / Bridge forward
- [ ] Read each explanation aloud — does it sound defensive? If so, reword to sound matter-of-fact
- [ ] Ensure you mention at least one productive activity during the gap
- [ ] Time the explanation — target 30-60 seconds, then pivot to the role
- [ ] Practice the pivot: end every gap explanation with "and that is why I am excited about this opportunity because..."
- [ ] Have someone ask you "What were you doing during this time?" and grade your composure

## Practice Phase
- [ ] Deliver your gap explanation aloud 3 times, varying the follow-up pivot
- [ ] Practice a mock interview where the gap question comes unexpectedly
- [ ] Record yourself and check for hesitation or over-explaining
