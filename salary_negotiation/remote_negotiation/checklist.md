# Cross-Border Remote Salary Negotiation — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to prepare for negotiating remote compensation with US and APAC companies as a Vietnam-based engineer.

## Preparation Steps

- [ ] Research the company's remote pay model before the first interview (location-agnostic, geo-tiered, country-band, or local entity)
- [ ] Identify which geographic tier the company assigns Vietnam to, if applicable
- [ ] Calculate your personal cost baseline: annual living expenses + self-funded health insurance ($1,000-$3,000/yr) + learning budget ($500-$2,000/yr) + equipment depreciation + retirement savings target
- [ ] Determine your minimum acceptable rate in USD using the framework: expenses + benefits gap + tax buffer + risk buffer (if contractor)
- [ ] Gather 3-5 comparable remote salary data points from Levels.fyi, VietnamDevs, Arc.dev, Wellfound, and peer networks
- [ ] Research the company's EOR provider (if applicable) to understand available benefits: Deel, Remote.com, Oyster have different benefit tiers
- [ ] Understand your Vietnam PIT obligations for the salary range you're targeting (5-35% progressive, non-residents 20% flat)
- [ ] Prepare a list of non-salary benefits to negotiate: equipment ($1,000-$3,000), learning budget ($500-$3,000/yr), internet allowance ($20-$50/mo), health insurance top-up
- [ ] Clarify your preferred payment method (Wise recommended) and research transfer fees for alternatives
- [ ] Document 3 unique value props that justify above-local-market compensation (time zone overlap, specific domain expertise, direct communication skills)
- [ ] Evaluate whether HKD (household business) registration is right for you: if your annual revenue exceeds ~$19.5K, the 7% flat tax (5% VAT + 2% PIT) is cheaper than personal PIT (5-35%). Note: the 0% VAT "software production" rate is for registered companies, not HKDs -- plan for 7%. Register at the district Economic Division if it fits your situation
- [ ] If using HKD structure, open a business bank account (Techcombank, VIB, or ACB) for receiving USD -- banks will require HKD registration certificate, tax code, and service contract

## Script Practice

- [ ] Practice asking about pay model early: "Before we discuss numbers, I'd like to understand how compensation is structured for remote employees in my region. Is there a geographic pay adjustment?"
- [ ] Practice the location-based pay pushback: "I understand there's a geographic adjustment, but I'd like to discuss the value I bring -- including time zone overlap and real-time collaboration with the US team."
- [ ] Practice clarifying employment structure: "Could you walk me through the employment structure? Will I be on a local entity through an EOR, or is this a contractor arrangement?"
- [ ] Practice asking about currency and payment: "What currency will compensation be paid in, and how is the exchange rate handled?"
- [ ] Practice the total package pivot: "Beyond base salary, I'd like to discuss the full package including equipment, learning budget, and health coverage."
- [ ] Practice declining to convert VND salary to USD: "I'd prefer to discuss compensation in terms of the role's scope and the global talent market, rather than converting my current compensation."
- [ ] Practice the equity gap negotiation: "I noticed equity isn't available for Vietnam-based employees. Could we bridge that gap with additional base or signing bonus?"

## Scenario Walkthroughs

- [ ] Role-play: US company offers $40K for a senior role citing "Vietnam rates" -- negotiate up using comparable data and time zone overlap value
- [ ] Role-play: Singapore company offers S$80K -- normalize to USD, compare total packages, and negotiate
- [ ] Role-play: Offer is contractor (B2B) with no benefits -- negotiate equipment stipend, learning budget, and higher gross rate (25-40% above employee rate)
- [ ] Role-play: Company says "all remote employees get the same benefits package" -- discover the package is US-only (401k, health insurance) and negotiate equivalent value for Vietnam
- [ ] Role-play: Recruiter asks "What's your current salary in VND?" -- practice declining without giving your number, redirect to market rate discussion
- [ ] Role-play: Equity offered but only as stock options with complex exercise mechanics -- ask the right questions (vesting, exercise window, Vietnam tax treatment, liquidity)
- [ ] Role-play: Two competing offers in different currencies (USD vs. SGD) -- normalize and compare total packages
- [ ] Role-play: Company requires a business entity (not personal payment) -- discuss HKD registration vs. LLC setup, and how to present your business details professionally
- [ ] Role-play: Explaining your tax structure (HKD 2% flat rate) to a US hiring manager who doesn't understand Vietnam tax, and why your gross rate can be lower than an employee rate while netting higher
- [ ] Role-play: Company wants to adjust your pay because you're moving to a different country -- negotiate grace period and clear rate-change formula
