# Questions for Interviewer — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to prepare thoughtful questions that demonstrate your interest, strategic thinking, and cultural awareness.
Prepare 5-8 questions and plan to ask 3-5 depending on the interview stage.

## Drafting Phase
- [ ] Write 2-3 questions about the Work (signals technical curiosity): hardest problems, architecture decisions, deployment pipeline
- [ ] Write 2-3 questions about the Team (signals collaboration awareness): work distribution, code reviews, engineering-product dynamic
- [ ] Write 1-2 questions about Growth (signals ambition): career progression, skills for success, professional development support
- [ ] Write 1-2 questions about Culture (signals self-awareness about fit): surprises about the company, handling deadlines, onboarding
- [ ] Tailor at least one question specifically to the interviewer's role or background
- [ ] Avoid questions whose answers are easily found on the company website or job posting

## Refinement Phase
- [ ] Read each question aloud — is it open-ended and conversational, not a yes/no question?
- [ ] Remove any question that could be perceived as confrontational or entitled
- [ ] Verify questions are tailored to the interviewer's role: hiring manager = strategy, engineer = technical decisions, recruiter = process
- [ ] Check that your strongest question is saved for last — interviewers remember the final exchange most clearly
- [ ] Prepare a strong closing question: "What skills or experiences would make someone exceptionally successful in this role after the first year?"
- [ ] Practice prioritizing which questions to ask when time is limited

## Practice Phase
- [ ] Role-play asking your top 5 questions with a friend acting as the interviewer
- [ ] Practice listening to the answer and asking one natural follow-up question per response
- [ ] Time a full Q&A round to 5-8 minutes
