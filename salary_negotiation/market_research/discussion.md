# Market Research

Gathering compensation data, understanding leveling, and positioning yourself in the market before you ever enter a negotiation.

## Key Concepts

- Compensation is not a secret -- data is available if you know where to look
- Your market value is not what you currently make; it is what employers are willing to pay for your skills today
- Leveling varies wildly between companies (a "Senior" at one company may be "Staff" at another)
- Geographic location, company size, and industry all create distinct compensation bands
- Total compensation often differs more than base salary when comparing companies
- Always research the company's specific leveling system before your first conversation

## Numbers & Benchmarks

- Software engineer median total compensation in the US (2024-2025): $120k-$250k depending on metro and level
- San Francisco/Bay Area typically commands 15-30% premium over other US metros for tech roles
- Level bump (e.g., mid-level to senior) typically adds 30-50% to total compensation at large tech companies
- FAANG/Large Cap tech total comp for mid-level: $200k-$350k; senior: $300k-$550k; staff: $450k-$800k+
- Non-tech Fortune 500 engineering roles typically pay 30-50% less than big tech for equivalent levels
- Remote roles from HCOL areas into LCOL areas: 5-15% reduction vs fully on-site HCOL positions
- Startup seed/Series A engineers: $100k-$180k base + 0.1%-1.5% equity depending on seniority and stage

## Common Pitfalls

- Relying on a single data source -- always cross-reference at least 3 sources
- Confusing title with level -- "VP of Engineering" at a 10-person startup is not the same as at a 10,000-person company
- Not adjusting for geography when comparing offers from different locations
- Sharing your current salary too early, which anchors the conversation to your past rather than your market value
- Ignoring total compensation and focusing only on base salary when benchmarking
- Using outdated data -- compensation bands shift significantly year over year in tech

## Scripts & Talking Points

- "Before we discuss numbers, I'd love to understand how your engineering levels are structured so I can make sure we're aligned on expectations."
- "Based on my research into comparable roles in this market, my understanding is that the range for this level is typically between $X and $Y. Does that align with your budget?"
- "I'm targeting a total compensation package in the range of $X to $Y, based on the data I have gathered from industry benchmarks and my conversations with peers in similar roles."
- "I'd prefer not to share my current compensation. I'm more interested in discussing what is appropriate for this role based on its scope and responsibilities."
- "Could you walk me through how leveling works here? I want to make sure I understand the growth trajectory so we can find the right fit."
