# Equity & RSU Evaluation — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to practice decoding equity offers and making informed comparisons across companies with different compensation structures.

## Preparation Steps

- [ ] Learn the difference between RSUs, stock options, and restricted stock -- be able to explain each in plain language
- [ ] Research the current 409A valuation and last preferred price for your target startup (if applicable)
- [ ] Build a model with best/base/worst-case scenarios for equity value at a pre-IPO company
- [ ] Compare the equity grant against public company RSUs by estimating the probability-weighted return
- [ ] Understand double-trigger vs. single-trigger acceleration and how it affects your risk
- [ ] Understand the 83(b) election: paying tax on options at grant date (when value is near zero) instead of at vesting -- only available for options, not RSUs

## Script Practice

- [ ] Practice asking: "Can you walk me through the current valuation, dilution expectations, and the strike price?"
- [ ] Practice saying: "I'd love to better understand the refresh policy -- how has equity been refreshed for engineers at my level historically?"
- [ ] Practice translating a stock option grant into a plain-language explanation of potential upside and downside
- [ ] Practice asking about double-trigger acceleration: "What happens to my equity in the event of an acquisition? Is there double-trigger acceleration?"
- [ ] Practice asking about exercise window and 83(b): "For the options, what is the exercise window if I leave the company? Is there early exercise availability, and does the company offer an 83(b) election?"

## Scenario Walkthroughs

- [ ] Role-play: Startup offers 0.1% in options with a $2B valuation -- evaluate whether this is competitive
- [ ] Role-play: Public company RSU grant has a 4-year cliff -- negotiate for quarterly vesting instead
- [ ] Role-play: Comparing a FAANG RSU package vs. a Series C startup option package -- articulate tradeoffs clearly
- [ ] Role-play: You exercise options and get hit with a massive AMT tax bill -- practice understanding and planning for AMT consequences before exercising
- [ ] Role-play: Evaluating equity at a private company with no near-term liquidity event -- practice assessing the risk of illiquidity and factoring it into your comp analysis
