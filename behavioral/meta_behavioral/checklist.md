# Meta Behavioral Interview Prep -- Working Checklist

Use this checklist each time you practice. Check items as you verbalize them.
The goal is to work through ALL items without referring to discussion.md.

## Phase 1: Requirements (2-3 min)
- [ ] Identify your target level (E4 / E5 / E6 / E7) and calibrate story scope accordingly
- [ ] Confirm you understand Meta's 4 behavioral evaluation dimensions:
  - [ ] Collaboration and Influence (most weighted at E5+)
  - [ ] Impact and Prioritization
  - [ ] Communication and Feedback
  - [ ] Growth Mindset and Failure Learning
- [ ] Know the key structural difference from Amazon: fewer, deeper questions (3 questions in 45 min), 2-4 follow-ups per story
- [ ] Understand the primary evaluation signal: scope of impact, not whether you "did the thing"
- [ ] Verify your story pool includes at least one number per story (Meta treats unmeasured impact as incomplete)

## Phase 2: High-Level Design (3-5 min)
- [ ] Map your 8-10 prepared stories to Meta's 4 dimensions using the Story Preparation Matrix:
  - [ ] Each story has a clear Primary Dimension
  - [ ] Each story has a scope level matching your target (E5 = team, E6 = org, E7 = company)
  - [ ] Each story has a quantified key metric to cite
- [ ] Verify every story passes the scope audit:
  - [ ] Did the approach get adopted by anyone outside your team?
  - [ ] Did you create a lasting artifact (doc, runbook, checklist, process) others now use?
  - [ ] Did your work change how the team or org operates going forward?
- [ ] Verify every story can withstand 3-4 follow-up probes:
  - [ ] "Why didn't you do X instead?"
  - [ ] "What would you change if you made that decision again?"
  - [ ] "How did you measure that?"
  - [ ] "What did your manager think of your approach?"

## Phase 3: Core Component Design (5-8 min)
- [ ] For each prepared story, verify the Collaboration and Influence dimension:
  - [ ] You understood the other party's constraints and incentives, not just advocated your own
  - [ ] You navigated disagreement, not avoided it
  - [ ] E6 signal: you changed how another team operates durably
- [ ] For each prepared story, verify the Impact and Prioritization dimension:
  - [ ] You named what you decided NOT to work on and why
  - [ ] You articulated the opportunity cost of your decisions
  - [ ] E6 signal: you set the prioritization framework for your team or org
- [ ] For each prepared story, verify the Communication and Feedback dimension:
  - [ ] You translated technical complexity into business terms
  - [ ] Feedback stories show integration, not defensiveness
  - [ ] E6 signal: you built a feedback culture on your team or org
- [ ] For each prepared story, verify the Growth Mindset and Failure Learning dimension:
  - [ ] Learning is specific and behavioral, not generic
  - [ ] You applied the learning in a subsequent situation
  - [ ] E6 signal: failure became a systemic fix preventing the same class of error for other teams
- [ ] Prepare your Move Fast story with the correct structure:
  - [ ] Feature flags + monitoring + rollback plan
  - [ ] "Shipped behind a feature flag to N% with a metric tripwire and auto-rollback"
  - [ ] NOT "we shipped without testing to hit a deadline" (that signals recklessness)

## Phase 4: Deep Dive (5-10 min, interviewer-directed)
- [ ] Scope amplification check -- confirm your E6-targeting stories add the E6 delta:
  - [ ] Recognizing the pattern applied beyond your team
  - [ ] Deliberately investing in cross-team propagation
  - [ ] Creating a lasting artifact that others now use
- [ ] Anti-pattern check -- confirm your stories avoid:
  - [ ] "Cross-functional collaboration" that is actually just communication ("I told other teams our decision")
  - [ ] Stories that only describe what you built, with no mention of what you deprioritized
  - [ ] Feedback stories ending with "they were wrong" or "I eventually showed them my approach worked"
  - [ ] Failure stories with generic learnings ("I learned to communicate better")
  - [ ] Move Fast stories that eliminated quality instead of adding safety mechanisms
- [ ] Red flag check per dimension:
  - [ ] Collaboration: no stories where influence = just communicating your team's decision
  - [ ] Prioritization: no stories missing what you deprioritized
  - [ ] Communication: no stories where you dismissed feedback
  - [ ] Failure: no stories where the failure is minimized or learning is vague
- [ ] Practice answering the 3 sample interview questions aloud:
  - [ ] "Tell me about a time you influenced a decision or outcome you didn't have direct authority over."
  - [ ] "Describe a product or technical decision you made that turned out to be wrong. What happened, and what specifically did you change?"
  - [ ] "How do you prioritize when everything feels urgent and you have more work than your team can handle?"

## Phase 5: Wrap-Up (2-3 min)
- [ ] Audit your 3 best stories for scope: if all three describe team-level impact, find a broader-scope story or add the organizational narrative
- [ ] Verify every answer includes at least one number -- "approximately" + reasonable estimate is acceptable
- [ ] Confirm your Move Fast story is ready with feature flag + rollback tripwire structure
- [ ] Know your target level and compensation range before the conversation (E5: $280K-$380K, E6: $380K-$550K)
- [ ] Prepare to ask: "What level does this role map to?" -- title is less informative than the level number
