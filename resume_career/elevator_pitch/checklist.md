# Elevator Pitch — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to craft and rehearse a concise self-introduction for networking events, career fairs, and the classic "Tell me about yourself" question.
Target 30-45 seconds spoken (roughly 60-90 words).

## Drafting Phase
- [ ] Write your current role and how long you have been in it (1 sentence)
- [ ] Identify 1-2 specific achievements with quantified results that align with the role you are targeting
- [ ] State what you are looking for and why this opportunity excites you (1 sentence)
- [ ] Combine into a single flowing paragraph, 60-90 words total

## Refinement Phase
- [ ] Read aloud and time it — target 30-45 seconds
- [ ] Verify the pitch follows the four-part formula: Current Identity, Key Strength, Proof Point, Forward Hook
- [ ] Check that the pitch does NOT start with personal history or biography (no "I grew up..." or "I always loved...")
- [ ] Remove any jargon or acronyms that a non-technical recruiter would not understand
- [ ] Ensure the pitch sounds conversational, not like you are reading a script
- [ ] Adjust the closing sentence for different contexts (networking vs. interview vs. LinkedIn)
- [ ] Test with a friend and ask: "Could you repeat back what I do?"

## Practice Phase
- [ ] Deliver the pitch aloud 5 times in a row without stopping
- [ ] Record yourself and listen back for filler words (um, like, so)
- [ ] Practice a single version — keep it under 60 seconds, do not memorize word-for-word
