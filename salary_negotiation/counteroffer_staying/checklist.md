# Counteroffer & Staying — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to prepare for counteroffer situations, whether you are leveraging an outside offer or deciding whether to accept one from your current employer.

## Preparation Steps

- [ ] Research industry data on counteroffer acceptance rates and 12-month retention statistics
- [ ] List the non-monetary reasons you considered leaving (growth, culture, manager, tech stack) and rank them
- [ ] Prepare a written summary of what you would need to stay, including comp and career development changes
- [ ] Identify your current manager's likely response style (accommodating, resistant, emotional) and plan accordingly
- [ ] Decide in advance whether you would accept a counteroffer at all -- establish a clear personal policy
- [ ] Review clawback provisions on your current signing bonus and relocation package before resigning -- understand what you would owe if you leave before the clawback date

## Script Practice

- [ ] Practice telling your manager: "I have received an offer from another company. Before I decide, I wanted to let you know and see if there is room to discuss my current role."
- [ ] Practice the "this is not about money" pivot: "Compensation is one factor, but I also need to feel confident about my growth trajectory here."
- [ ] Practice declining a counteroffer graciously: "I truly appreciate the offer to stay. This was a difficult decision, but I have committed to the new role."
- [ ] Practice the knowledge transfer/transition script: "I am happy to help with the transition. Let me put together a knowledge transfer document and ensure my projects are handed off smoothly before my last day."
- [ ] Practice the clawback clarification script: "I want to make sure I understand the clawback provisions on my signing bonus. Can you clarify what I would owe if I leave before [date]?"

## Scenario Walkthroughs

- [ ] Role-play: Your current company matches the outside offer -- decide whether to accept or decline and explain why
- [ ] Role-play: Your manager gets defensive and questions your loyalty -- stay professional and redirect to facts
- [ ] Role-play: Your current company offers more money but no changes to the issues that made you want to leave
- [ ] Role-play: You resign, then receive a counteroffer the next day -- evaluate it against your original criteria
- [ ] Role-play: You leave without requesting a reference or LinkedIn recommendation before your last day -- practice ensuring you secure references while relationships are still warm
