# Screening & Hiring AI Engineers -- Prep Checklist

Use this checklist to prepare for evaluating AI engineering candidates or for understanding how you'll be assessed as a candidate.

## For Interviewers / Hiring Managers

- [ ] **Define competency framework**: Write explicit competency areas and weights before designing any questions.
- [ ] **Write role-specific JD**: Describe actual week-to-week work, not a wishlist combining 4 roles.
- [ ] **Design 4-stage process**: Recruiter screen, technical screen, deep technical, stakeholder/behavioral.
- [ ] **Build scoring rubrics**: Define 1-4 scale with anchor descriptions for each competency.
- [ ] **Design or select take-home assignment**: Keep to 2-4 hours, relevant to actual work, include eval criteria.
- [ ] **Prepare defence round structure**: Plan 45-90 min walkthrough with specific questions about design decisions.
- [ ] **Create red flag / green flag reference**: Share with all interviewers to ensure consistent evaluation.
- [ ] **Train interviewers**: Cover probing questions, rubric usage, avoiding anchoring, legal requirements.
- [ ] **Set AI tool policy**: Explicitly state whether candidates can use AI in take-homes and live interviews.
- [ ] **Define calibrated debrief process**: Independent scores first, discuss divergences, document decisions.
- [ ] **Set 90-day success criteria**: Define what the hire needs to accomplish in first 3 and 6 months.
- [ ] **Distinguish builders vs. talkers**: Prepare diagnostic questions that reveal production experience depth.
- [ ] **Avoid pedigree trap**: Focus on production evidence, not FAANG/PhD credentials.
- [ ] **Prepare system design questions**: Design domain-specific AI system design prompts relevant to your stack.

## For Candidates (Understanding How You're Evaluated)

- [ ] **Know the 4-stage structure**: Understand what each stage tests and prepare accordingly.
- [ ] **Prepare war stories**: Be ready to describe real production failures and how you fixed them.
- [ ] **Know your numbers**: Cost-per-request, latency, throughput, user count for systems you've built.
- [ ] **Practice think-aloud reasoning**: Externalize your thought process during technical discussions.
- [ ] **Build eval-first mentality**: Always start with evaluation before building the main system.
- [ ] **Document design decisions**: Be able to explain why you chose approach X over alternatives Y and Z.
- [ ] **Prepare for defence round**: Practice walking through your architecture and tradeoffs.
- [ ] **Demonstrate AI collaboration literacy**: Show you can use AI tools effectively without depending on them.
- [ ] **Understand the builder vs. talker distinction**: Interviewers are actively screening for this.
- [ ] **Prepare for the new format**: AI-paired coding, reasoning labs, ethics rounds are becoming standard.
- [ ] **Connect technical metrics to business outcomes**: Frame your work in terms of business impact.
- [ ] **Know your eval tools**: Be ready to discuss RAGAS, Langfuse, Braintrust, or custom pipelines.

## Practice Questions

1. Design a 45-minute technical screen for an AI engineer with 2 years of experience. What 3 questions do you ask and why?
2. Design a take-home RAG assignment. What do you provide, what do you evaluate, and how do you prevent plagiarism?
3. A candidate has a polished GitHub with AI projects. What 3 things do you look for to determine if they actually built them?
4. What is the difference between an "AI talker" and an "AI builder"? Give 3 diagnostic questions that distinguish them.
5. Design a scoring rubric for an AI engineer system design interview (RAG system). What are the 4 scoring dimensions?
6. What are the top 3 red flags when screening AI engineers? For each, give an example and why it's concerning.
7. How would you structure a multi-round interview process for a senior AI engineer? What does each round evaluate?
8. Design a pair programming exercise for evaluating prompt engineering skills. What scenario and what do you observe?
9. A candidate excels in algorithm questions but cannot explain their RAG architecture decisions. What does this signal?
10. How do you evaluate a candidate's production AI experience when their previous work is under NDA?
11. Design an evaluation framework for comparing two AI engineer candidates. What dimensions matter beyond technical skill?
12. Walk through your process for calibrating scores across 4 interviewers after a day of AI engineer interviews.
