# Amazon Leadership Principles Interview Prep -- Working Checklist

Use this checklist each time you practice. Check items as you verbalize them.
The goal is to work through ALL items without referring to discussion.md.

## Phase 1: Requirements (2-3 min)
- [ ] Identify which LPs this role emphasizes (check the job description)
- [ ] Confirm your level target (SDE II / Senior / Staff / Principal) and calibrate story scope
- [ ] List the 5 most-probed LPs for your level:
  - [ ] Ownership (SDE II+)
  - [ ] Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit (Staff/Principal signal)
  - [ ] Think Big (Senior to Principal)
  - [ ] Dive Deep (SDE II+, especially EM/TL)
  - [ ] Insist on the Highest Standards (SDE II+)
- [ ] Verify your story pool covers all 16 LPs (8-10 stories, each mapping to 2-3 LPs)
- [ ] Know the scoring rubric: 3 = positive story with result, 4 = + adversity + tradeoffs + reflection, 5 = + scope beyond level + changed future behavior

## Phase 2: High-Level Design (3-5 min)
- [ ] Map your 8-10 prepared stories to LPs using the Story Preparation Matrix:
  - [ ] Each story has a clear Primary LP and Secondary LP
  - [ ] Each story has a level signal matching your target
  - [ ] Each story has a quantified key result
- [ ] Verify every story passes the "I" test: decisions and actions have "I" as the subject, not "we"
- [ ] Verify every story passes the scope test: at least one story shows you acting BEYOND your authority
- [ ] Verify every story has adversity: a moment where it could have gone wrong and your choices made it go right
- [ ] Confirm each story follows the asymmetric STAR time allocation:
  - [ ] Situation: 45-60 seconds (20%) -- stakes and "why this mattered"
  - [ ] Task: 20-30 seconds (10%) -- your specific responsibility in one sentence
  - [ ] Action: 90-120 seconds (50%) -- alternatives rejected, friction navigated, decisions owned
  - [ ] Result: 45-60 seconds (20%) -- quantified business impact + "what I'd do differently"

## Phase 3: Core Component Design (5-8 min)
- [ ] For each prepared story, verify the Action section contains:
  - [ ] At least one alternative you considered and rejected (with reason)
  - [ ] A moment of friction or pushback you navigated
  - [ ] A decision that was yours to make (not the team's or manager's)
- [ ] For each prepared story, verify the Result section contains:
  - [ ] At least one quantified metric (NPS, latency, cost, time saved, etc.)
  - [ ] A "so what" -- the outcome connected to customer or business impact
  - [ ] A "what I'd do differently" -- specific, behavioral, not generic
- [ ] Prepare your Disagree and Commit story with all three elements:
  - [ ] Genuine disagreement articulated before the decision (with data)
  - [ ] The decision went against you (you LOST the argument)
  - [ ] You committed fully and visibly (execution indistinguishable from a supported decision)
- [ ] Prepare follow-up answers for Disagree and Commit:
  - [ ] "What did you say to your team after the decision was announced?"
  - [ ] "How did you show up in the first team meeting afterward?"
- [ ] Verify your Ownership story goes beyond assigned scope:
  - [ ] Something your manager did NOT ask you to fix
  - [ ] Preferably a production failure starting in another team's code

## Phase 4: Deep Dive (5-10 min, interviewer-directed)
- [ ] Anti-pattern check -- confirm your stories avoid all five:
  - [ ] No collective language ("we built" without specifying YOUR decision)
  - [ ] No stories entirely within your authority (must go beyond scope)
  - [ ] No positive-only stories without adversity
  - [ ] No "I convinced them" masquerading as Disagree and Commit
  - [ ] No vague learning ("I learned to communicate better"); must be specific and behavioral
- [ ] Level-specific signal check:
  - [ ] Customer Obsession: collected customer data BEFORE the solution was defined
  - [ ] Invent and Simplify: innovation made system easier for next engineer, not just more powerful
  - [ ] Are Right, A Lot: changed position based on data in a high-stakes situation
  - [ ] Learn and Be Curious: self-directed learning directly unblocked a project
  - [ ] Dive Deep: personally pulled logs/ran queries/audited code, found something the summary missed
  - [ ] Frugality: constraint produced a permanently better solution than unconstrained version
  - [ ] Earn Trust: proactively surfaced a problem before being asked, even with personal risk
- [ ] Prepare to handle LP-misalignment:
  - [ ] If unsure which LP a question maps to, ask: "Could you tell me more about what you're looking to understand?"
  - [ ] Know how to pivot a story to hit a secondary LP if the primary doesn't match
- [ ] Practice redirecting when the interviewer probes a story you've already used:
  - [ ] Have a backup story for each of the top-5 LPs

## Phase 5: Wrap-Up (2-3 min)
- [ ] Time yourself during practice: Situation < 60s, Action is the longest section
- [ ] Summarize your story delivery readiness: all 8-10 stories practiced aloud within time limits
- [ ] Review your story-to-LP mapping one final time before the interview
- [ ] Plan your opening: know which LP each interviewer likely owns and lead with a matching story
- [ ] When telling a story, name the LP connection explicitly: "This story is most relevant to Ownership because..."
