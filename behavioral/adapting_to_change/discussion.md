# Adapting to Changing Requirements

## What Interviewers Look For
- Whether you treat change as a normal part of software development, not a personal annoyance
- Your ability to re-prioritize quickly when the landscape shifts
- Evidence that you communicate proactively when plans change (to manager, team, stakeholders)
- Whether you ask "why" to understand the business context behind the change

## STAR Framework Refresher
| Letter | Meaning | Guiding Question |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| S | Situation | Set the scene in 1-2 sentences |
| T | Task | What was your responsibility or goal? |
| A | Action | What specific steps did YOU take? (use "I", not "we") |
| R | Result | What was the outcome? (quantify if possible) |

## Common Mistakes
- Complaining about the change or framing stakeholders as unreasonable for changing their minds
- Not asking WHY the requirement changed -- understanding the business driver is crucial context
- Tunnel vision: continuing to build toward the original plan despite clear signals it no longer fits
- Failing to communicate the impact of the change to your manager or the team in terms of timeline or scope

## Story Mining Prompts
Think about these situations from your experience:
- A major pivot mid-project where the product direction changed significantly
- A new requirement arrived that invalidated weeks of work you had already completed
- A team reorg that changed your reporting structure, team composition, or project assignment
- Scope creep on a project where stakeholders kept adding features without adjusting the timeline
- A change in business strategy (e.g., pivot from B2C to B2B) that required rethinking your technical approach

## Good vs Bad Examples

**Bad:** "The PM kept changing the requirements and it was frustrating, but we just did what they asked and shipped it eventually."

This is bad because it shows passive acceptance with no proactive communication or attempt to understand the reasoning behind the changes.

**Good:** "We were three weeks into building a customer-facing dashboard when leadership pivoted our target audience from enterprise to SMB. The data model we designed assumed complex multi-tenant hierarchies that SMBs did not need. I scheduled a 30-minute session with the PM to understand the new requirements, then proposed we simplify the data model and reuse 60% of our existing API layer. I presented the revised plan with updated estimates to the team. We delivered the SMB version in four weeks instead of the original six-week plan, and the simplified architecture reduced our query latency by 40%."

## Variations to Prepare
- "Tell me about a time the requirements changed significantly on a project."
- "Describe a situation where you had to adapt quickly to new information."
- "Give me an example of how you handle scope creep."
- "Have you ever had to throw away work because of a changing direction? How did you respond?"
