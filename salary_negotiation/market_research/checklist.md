# Market Research — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to ensure you walk into any negotiation armed with accurate, defensible compensation data.

## Preparation Steps

- [ ] Gather salary ranges from at least 3 sources (e.g., Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, industry surveys, and peer conversations)
- [ ] Identify 3-5 peers at similar companies with comparable YOE and collect their comp bands
- [ ] Adjust market data for your specific location using a cost-of-labor index
- [ ] Document your top 3 unique value props that justify targeting above-median comp
- [ ] Build a one-page "comp brief" summarizing your research findings and target range

## Script Practice

- [ ] Practice stating your target number with a confident, data-backed rationale (out loud, 3x)
- [ ] Practice the phrase: "Based on my research into comparable roles at similar companies, the range I'm seeing is..."
- [ ] Practice pivoting from a single data point to a broader market narrative when challenged
- [ ] Practice declining to share current salary: "I'd prefer not to share my current compensation. I'm more interested in discussing what is appropriate for this role based on its scope and responsibilities."
- [ ] Practice asking about leveling: "Before we discuss numbers, I'd love to understand how your engineering levels are structured so I can make sure we're aligned on expectations."

## Scenario Walkthroughs

- [ ] Role-play: Recruiter says your number is "above band" -- walk through responding with evidence
- [ ] Role-play: Hiring manager asks where you got your data -- practice citing multiple credible sources
- [ ] Role-play: You discover mid-conversation that the role level is lower than you assumed -- recalibrate
