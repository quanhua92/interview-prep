# Initial Offer Evaluation — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to practice receiving, analyzing, and responding to an initial offer without committing prematurely.

## Preparation Steps

- [ ] Create a scoring rubric with weighted criteria (comp, growth, team, WLB, commute) to evaluate offers objectively
- [ ] Pre-write a list of 5-7 questions to ask before accepting any offer (vesting details, review cycle, promotion criteria)
- [ ] Prepare a template "thank you + clarifying questions" email to send within 24 hours of receiving an offer
- [ ] Set a personal rule to never accept or reject an offer on the first call -- always ask for time
- [ ] Practice computing 4-year total comp (not just annualized TC) for a sample offer -- include base x4, RSU, signing, bonus x4, and refreshers
- [ ] Practice decomposing an offer into all components and identifying which are negotiable vs. fixed by policy
- [ ] Identify your BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement) before evaluating any offer
- [ ] Factor in commute-cost differences ($5k-$15k/year between remote, hybrid, and on-site roles) when comparing offers

## Script Practice

- [ ] Practice saying: "Thank you for the offer. I'm excited about the opportunity. When do you need a decision by?"
- [ ] Practice saying: "I'd like to review the full details and discuss with my family. Can I get back to you by [date]?"
- [ ] Practice following up with: "I have a few clarifying questions about the equity structure and signing bonus timeline."
- [ ] Practice the equity details clarification script: "How does the equity grant work? What is the current strike price, vesting schedule, and are there any acceleration clauses?"
- [ ] Practice the "never give your number first" response: "I'm really focused on finding the right role. Can you tell me what the total compensation package looks like for this position?"
- [ ] Practice the component-specific negotiation script: "Could we look at the signing bonus or RSU grant instead? An additional $X in signing would bridge the gap significantly."
- [ ] Practice the Round 2 push script: "Thank you for coming back with [counter]. I'm still $[gap] away from making this an easy decision."

## Scenario Walkthroughs

- [ ] Role-play: Verbal offer comes in lower than expected -- stay calm, express enthusiasm, ask for written details
- [ ] Role-play: Recruiter pressures you to decide within 24 hours -- practice buying time professionally
- [ ] Role-play: Offer looks good on the surface but equity details are vague -- practice requesting a breakdown
- [ ] Role-play: Recruiter asks "What are you looking for in total compensation?" -- practice deflecting without giving your number first
- [ ] Role-play: You have a competing offer -- practice the competing offer script with specific company name and TC
- [ ] Role-play: Base is at band ceiling -- practice pivoting to signing bonus negotiation
- [ ] Role-play: Compare a FAANG offer vs. a startup offer with equity -- practice applying equity haircuts (20-35% for late private, 50-70% for early startup)
