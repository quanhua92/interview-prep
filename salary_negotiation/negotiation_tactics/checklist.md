# Negotiation Tactics — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to internalize core negotiation frameworks and practice deploying them under pressure.

## Preparation Steps

- [ ] Write down your BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement) and know your walk-away number
- [ ] Prepare an anchor number 15-25% above your actual target to create room for concession
- [ ] List 3 non-salary items you can request as leverage (remote days, title, signing bonus, accelerated review)
- [ ] Map out a concession plan: what you will ask for first, and what you will "give up" to reach agreement
- [ ] Review the "let me think about it" pause technique -- practice staying silent for 5 seconds after any offer
- [ ] Commit to getting every agreement in writing -- verbal commitments from recruiters are not binding
- [ ] Identify the person with budget authority and negotiate directly with them, not through a middleman

## Script Practice

- [ ] Practice the anchoring opener: "I was hoping we could get closer to $X given my experience with..."
- [ ] Practice the silence technique: receive a lowball number, pause, then respond calmly
- [ ] Practice the "package deal" pivot: "If base is firm, could we explore a signing bonus or additional equity to close the gap?"
- [ ] Practice the competing offer script: "I have another offer on the table for $X total compensation. I would strongly prefer to join your team. Is there anything we can do to get closer to that number?"

## Scenario Walkthroughs

- [ ] Role-play: Company says "this is our final offer" -- practice the "what would it take to revisit?" follow-up
- [ ] Role-play: Recruiter asks for your current salary -- practice deflecting to your expectations
- [ ] Role-play: Negotiation stalls at $10k apart -- practice splitting the difference with a non-monetary addition
- [ ] Role-play: You get everything you ask for -- practice accepting graciously without second-guessing
- [ ] Role-play: You catch yourself apologizing for negotiating ("I'm sorry to ask, but...") -- rephrase with confidence and no apology
- [ ] Role-play: Recruiter tries to conduct the entire negotiation over email -- practice requesting a phone or video call to build rapport and read reactions in real time
