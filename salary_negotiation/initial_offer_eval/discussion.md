# Initial Offer Evaluation

How to read an offer letter, identify all negotiation levers, and compare multiple offers using a structured scoring framework.

## Key Concepts

- An offer letter is a starting point, not a final statement -- every component is potentially negotiable
- You cannot negotiate what you do not understand -- read the full offer including all attached documents
- Identify your BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement) before evaluating any offer
- Use a scoring framework to compare offers objectively rather than relying on gut feeling
- Always evaluate the present value of equity, not just the grant size (consider vesting schedule, strike price, and company trajectory)
- The person extending the offer is often not the same person who approves the budget -- understand the chain

## Numbers & Benchmarks

- Typical time to evaluate and respond to an offer: 3-7 business days is standard and reasonable
- First offer is typically 10-20% below the approved budget -- companies expect negotiation
- Candidates who negotiate salary see an average increase of $5k-$15k; those who negotiate multiple components see $20k-$50k+ in additional value
- Offer acceptance rate at most companies: 70-85%, which means they have room to improve terms to win you
- When comparing offers, assign weights: 40% TC, 20% growth/learning, 15% team/culture, 15% WLB, 10% commute/flexibility (adjust to your priorities)
- Always request offers in writing -- verbal offers are not binding and details can shift

## Common Pitfalls

- Accepting the first offer out of excitement or pressure -- always take time to review
- Not reading the equity agreement, which may contain cliffs, clawback provisions, or unusual vesting terms
- Focusing on only one lever (usually base salary) when multiple levers can be adjusted
- Not asking for clarification on vague terms like "competitive bonus" or "equity commensurate with level"
- Failing to consider the cost of benefits differences between companies (premiums, deductibles, OOP maximums)
- Forgetting to factor in commute costs, which can differ by $5k-$15k/year between remote, hybrid, and on-site roles

## Scripts & Talking Points

- "Thank you for the offer. I am excited about the opportunity and want to give it the thoughtful review it deserves. When do you need a response?"
- "Could you send me the full offer package including the equity agreement and benefits summary so I can review everything together?"
- "I have a few questions about the offer. Can you clarify the bonus structure -- is the target guaranteed, and what percentage of employees typically receive the full amount?"
- "How does the equity grant work? What is the current strike price, vesting schedule, and are there any acceleration clauses?"
- "I'd like to discuss the offer further. Based on my research and experience, I was expecting a package closer to $X. Is there flexibility to get there?"
