# Difficult Scenarios — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to rehearse the toughest negotiation situations so you are never caught off guard.

## Preparation Steps

- [ ] Identify your top 3 most feared negotiation scenarios and write one-paragraph responses for each
- [ ] Prepare a "hard no" response that preserves the relationship without burning bridges
- [ ] Draft a respectful decline script for when an offer truly does not meet your minimum requirements
- [ ] Create a "pause and redirect" tactic for when a negotiator uses pressure or emotional appeals
- [ ] Research your legal rights regarding pay transparency laws in your state
- [ ] Prepare for entry-level negotiation: identify what you can negotiate (start date, team placement, signing bonus) even with less leverage
- [ ] Prepare an escalation strategy for "final offer" -- know who to ask next and how to frame the request professionally

## Script Practice

- [ ] Practice saying: "I appreciate the offer, but I cannot accept at that level. If there is flexibility, I would love to continue the conversation."
- [ ] Practice responding to "We don't negotiate" with: "I understand. Could we revisit the equity or signing bonus instead?"
- [ ] Practice walking away politely: "This role sounds excellent, but the compensation gap is too significant for me to make it work."
- [ ] Practice the escalating "final offer" script: "I understand this is your final offer at this level. Who would be the right person to discuss whether there is additional budget available?"
- [ ] Practice the entry-level negotiation script: "As a recent graduate, I am excited about this opportunity. I was hoping we could discuss the starting salary -- I had targeted $X based on my research. Even a small adjustment would help me feel confident in my decision."

## Scenario Walkthroughs

- [ ] Role-play: Manager says "take it or leave it, we have other candidates" -- maintain composure and restate value
- [ ] Role-play: You are tempted to take a significant pay cut for a "better" company without a clear ROI timeline -- practice calculating when the pay cut makes sense and when it does not
- [ ] Role-play: Two competing offers, and Company A demands a decision before Company B's offer arrives
- [ ] Role-play: Walking away from a lowball offer -- maintain professionalism and preserve the relationship for future opportunities
- [ ] Role-play: You are asked about your current salary early in the process -- practice deflecting to your market-based expectations using the "I'd prefer not to share my current compensation" script
