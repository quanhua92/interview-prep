# Working Under Pressure and Meeting Deadlines

## What Interviewers Look For
- Whether you can stay composed and make rational decisions when time is scarce
- Evidence of smart prioritization -- cutting the right scope, not cutting corners on quality
- Your ability to communicate trade-offs to stakeholders (what you can deliver vs. what they want)
- Whether you delegate effectively or try to do everything yourself under pressure

## STAR Framework Refresher
| Letter | Meaning | Guiding Question |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| S | Situation | Set the scene in 1-2 sentences |
| T | Task | What was your responsibility or goal? |
| A | Action | What specific steps did YOU take? (use "I", not "we") |
| R | Result | What was the outcome? (quantify if possible) |

## Common Mistakes
- Sacrificing quality silently without flagging the risk to stakeholders or your manager
- Romanticizing overwork ("I slept under my desk for three days") -- this signals poor planning, not heroism
- Accepting an impossible deadline without pushing back or negotiating scope
- Poor prioritization: working on low-impact tasks first because they are easier under stress

## Story Mining Prompts
Think about these situations from your experience:
- A deadline that seemed impossible and how you figured out what was actually feasible
- Multiple urgent deadlines hitting at the same time and how you triaged
- A critical production issue that surfaced during a release or major milestone
- A situation where you had to deliver a high-stakes project with fewer resources than planned
- A time you had to work with a fixed, non-negotiable deadline (compliance, legal, contractual)

## Good vs Bad Examples

**Bad:** "We had a really tight deadline so the whole team worked overtime for two weeks and we barely made it."

This is bad because it shows no strategic thinking, no prioritization, and frames sustained overwork as the solution.

**Good:** "We had a contractual deadline to deliver an API integration in three weeks, and the initial estimate was five weeks. I listed every feature, ranked them by business impact, and proposed to the PM that we deliver a phased release: core endpoints in week three and advanced filtering in week five. I also identified two tasks I could parallelize by pairing with a teammate. We hit the week-three deadline with the core features, and the customer signed the contract. The second phase shipped on schedule two weeks later with no issues."

## Variations to Prepare
- "Tell me about a time you had to meet a very tight deadline."
- "Describe a situation where you felt overwhelmed by competing demands. How did you handle it?"
- "Give me an example of a time you had to deliver results under pressure."
- "How do you handle it when you realize a deadline is at risk?"
