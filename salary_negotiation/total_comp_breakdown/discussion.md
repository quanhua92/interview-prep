# Total Compensation Breakdown

Understanding every component of an offer -- base salary, bonus, equity, benefits, and hidden value -- so you can evaluate and negotiate the full picture.

## Key Concepts

- Total compensation (TC) = base salary + annual bonus + equity (annual grant value) + benefits value
- Base salary is the most visible but often the least negotiable component at large tech companies
- Equity and bonuses are where the largest swings happen in negotiation
- Signing bonuses are one-time cash that can offset a lower base or make up for unvested equity at your current employer
- Benefits can be worth $15k-$40k/year but are frequently overlooked in negotiations
- Always normalize offers to an annual total comp number for apples-to-apples comparison

## Numbers & Benchmarks

- Typical base-to-TC ratio at large tech companies: 50-65% base, 10-20% bonus, 20-40% equity
- Annual bonus targets: 10-15% at non-tech companies, 15-25% at large tech, variable at startups
- 401(k) match: typically 3-6% of salary; some companies (e.g., generous startups) offer up to 10%
- Health insurance premiums: employer contribution typically worth $8k-$20k/year for family coverage
- Signing bonus ranges: $10k-$50k for mid-level, $30k-$150k+ for senior/staff at competitive companies
- Relocation packages: $10k-$30k domestic, $30k-$80k+ international; some companies offer lump sum
- Tuition reimbursement: $5k-$15k/year at companies that offer it
- Paid time off: 15-25 days standard; unlimited PTO is often worth less than it sounds (studies show people take less)

## Common Pitfalls

- Comparing only base salary and ignoring equity -- a $180k base + $120k equity beats a $200k base + $40k equity
- Not asking about equity refresh grants -- initial grant may look great but may not be refreshed annually
- Assuming bonus targets are guaranteed -- most bonuses are 0-200% of target based on performance
- Forgetting to account for tax differences between cash compensation and equity compensation
- Not negotiating signing bonus even when base salary is "capped" -- signing bonus often has more flexibility
- Overvaluing "unlimited PTO" and "free snacks" when those cost the employer almost nothing

## Scripts & Talking Points

- "I'd like to understand the full compensation structure. Can you break down the base, bonus target, equity grant, and any signing bonus for this offer?"
- "The base salary is close to what I was expecting, but I'd love to explore whether there is room on the signing bonus or equity grant to bring the total package in line with my target of $X."
- "Can you walk me through how equity refresh grants work? I want to understand the total value over my first four years."
- "Is there flexibility on the 401(k) match or any other benefits that could help close the gap between your offer and my other opportunities?"
- "I have approximately $X in unvested equity at my current company. Could we discuss a signing bonus to help offset that?"
