# Total Comp Breakdown — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to practice analyzing and articulating every component of a compensation package, not just base salary.

## Preparation Steps

- [ ] Build a personal total-comp spreadsheet with line items for base, bonus, equity, signing bonus, and benefits
- [ ] Research the vesting schedule, cliff period, and strike price (if applicable) for your target company
- [ ] Calculate the present value of equity grants using a 4-year vesting assumption
- [ ] Quantify non-monetary perks (401k match, health insurance differential, tuition reimbursement, PTO value) in dollar terms
- [ ] Identify which comp components are negotiable vs. fixed at your target company
- [ ] Research relocation package norms for your target company ($10k-$30k domestic, $30k-$80k+ international)
- [ ] Evaluate PTO policies critically -- "unlimited PTO" often results in employees taking less time off than a fixed allotment

## Script Practice

- [ ] Practice walking through your full comp breakdown clearly and concisely (out loud, 3x)
- [ ] Practice the phrase: "I'd like to understand the full picture -- can you walk me through bonus targets and equity refreshers?"
- [ ] Practice comparing two offers using annualized total comp rather than headline base salary
- [ ] Practice the unvested-equity offset signing bonus script: "I have approximately $X in unvested equity at my current company. Could we discuss a signing bonus to help offset that?"

## Scenario Walkthroughs

- [ ] Role-play: Explaining why Offer A ($140k base, $80k equity) is stronger than Offer B ($160k base, $20k equity)
- [ ] Role-play: Recruiter only wants to discuss base salary -- redirect to total comp conversation
- [ ] Role-play: Evaluating a startup offer with a low base but significant options -- calculate scenarios
- [ ] Role-play: Company offers "unlimited PTO" and free snacks to justify below-market base salary -- recognize the low real value of these perks
