# Career Narrative — Practice Checklist

Use this checklist to build a coherent story that connects your past roles, growth, and motivations into a compelling career arc.
A strong narrative answers "Why are you here, and where are you going?"

## Drafting Phase
- [ ] Map your career timeline: list every role with dates and a one-line purpose for each transition
- [ ] Identify the common thread or theme across your roles (e.g., scaling systems, leading teams, user-centric design)
- [ ] Write 2-3 sentences explaining why you chose each major career move
- [ ] Identify one pivotal moment that changed your career direction
- [ ] Draft a closing statement about what you want to do next and why
- [ ] Prepare a one-sentence version of your narrative for networking events

## Refinement Phase
- [ ] Read the full narrative aloud — does it tell a logical, engaging story?
- [ ] Check that each act follows the discussion's timing: Origin 15-20s, Progression 60-90s, Direction 20-30s (~2 min total)
- [ ] Verify the narrative includes non-technical growth (leadership, collaboration, impact on others) — not just technical skills
- [ ] Check that career transitions are framed positively ("I moved to learn X") rather than negatively ("I left because I was bored")
- [ ] Remove any tangents that do not support your central theme
- [ ] Ensure transitions between roles highlight growth, not random jumps
- [ ] Check that your motivation for the target role is clearly connected to your past
- [ ] Ask a colleague: "Does this narrative make you want to hire me?"

## Practice Phase
- [ ] Deliver the narrative aloud in under 3 minutes
- [ ] Practice answering a follow-up: "Why did you leave your last role?"
- [ ] Record yourself and listen for confidence and natural pacing
